First Night — Phoenix

5:00 PM
Easy Hike
Hole in the Rock — Papago Park
A gentle 20-minute scramble with iconic views of the Phoenix skyline framed through red sandstone. Perfect for first evening energy — low effort, very high reward. Free parking. Opens 5 AM, closes 7 PM.

June 19 · Thursday

Water Day

8:00 AM
Jet Ski
Jet Ski AZ Rentals — Lake Pleasant
Book in advance at jetskiazrentals.com — they haul the jet skis directly to the lake, no truck needed. 5-star rated. Go as early as possible before temps hit 110°F. Budget ~$250 for a 2-hour rental for two.
1:00 – 3:00 PM
Rest + Cool Down
Midday Break
Head back to the hotel. Escape the peak heat (1–4 PM is brutal). Pool time, nap, grab lunch at a nearby spot.
4:30 PM
Swimming Hole
Fossil Creek Waterfall
⚠️ Book a permit NOW at recreation.gov — $6/person, sells out weeks ahead for June. One of the most spectacular natural swimming holes in the US — turquoise spring-fed water surrounded by canyon walls. ~1.5 hrs from Phoenix. High-clearance vehicle recommended for the last stretch of road.
8:00 PM
Rooftop Drinks
Outrider Rooftop Lounge — Old Town Scottsdale
7th-floor rooftop with sweeping views of Camelback Mountain. Fire pits, cocktails, great vibes. Perfect sunset spot after a full water day.

June 20 · Friday

Sedona — ~2 hr drive from Phoenix

6:30 AM
~2 hrs from PHX
Iconic Hike
Devil's Bridge Trailhead
Arrive by 6:30–7 AM sharp. This is the must-do Sedona hike — a natural sandstone arch with jaw-dropping views. The line for the bridge photo can hit 45+ minutes by 9 AM. Moderate scrambling, ~4 miles round trip. Worth every step.
10:00 AM
Canyon Hike
Boynton Canyon Trail
Classic red rock canyon hike with a hidden Subway Cave at the top — secret chamber with 360° views. ~6 miles out and back, moderate. Finish by noon before the heat peaks. Look out for deer on the trail.
12:30 – 4:30 PM
Lunch + Rest
Explore Sedona Town + Lunch
Wander uptown Sedona, grab lunch at one of the local spots (Elote Cafe is a local favourite if you can snag a table). Browse the galleries and shops during the hot midday hours.
5:00 PM
Evening Hike
West Fork Oak Creek Trailhead
One of the most beautiful evening hikes in Arizona — a shaded canyon walk through towering red cliffs with 13 creek crossings. ~6.5 miles total, mostly flat. Bring waterproof shoes or sandals you don't mind getting wet. $15 parking fee per vehicle.
04

June 21 · Saturday

The Crack + Back to Phoenix

7:00 AM
Slot Canyon Swim
The Crack at Wet Beaver Creek
Start no later than 7 AM. A stunning natural slot canyon with a deep swimming and cliff-jumping hole at the end — 3.5 miles in. Finish hiking out before noon to avoid the sun on the exposed return. Bring 2L+ water per person, snacks, and sunscreen. On the drive back from Sedona to Phoenix — perfect stopover. Flash flood risk: check weather before going.
2:00 – 5:00 PM
Downtime
Drive back to Scottsdale + Rest
~1.5 hr drive back to Phoenix area. Shower, relax, grab a late lunch. You've earned it — this is your big rest block.
7:00 PM
Evening Out
Cottontail Lounge — W Scottsdale
Rooftop pool lounge at the W Hotel in Old Town Scottsdale. Mid-century chic design, daybeds, marble bar, weekend DJ sets. Great way to wind down the last big night. Old Town is walkable from here — explore the strip after.

Camelback + Fly Home

5:00 AM
Bucket List Hike
Echo Canyon Trailhead — Camelback Mountain
Set your alarm. Be on trail by 5 AM. The most iconic urban hike in Phoenix — intense rock scrambling, 1,280 ft elevation gain, and unreal 360° city views at the top. 2–3 hours round trip. Opens at 5 AM. Gets dangerously hot and crowded by 8 AM. Parking fills up — arrive early. No dogs allowed.

☀️ Survival Tips

🌡️ Heat is real. Expect 105–115°F by midday in late June. Every outdoor activity here is scheduled before 10 AM or after 5 PM for a reason.
💧 Hydration tips . Bring at least 2L of water per person per hike, more for The Crack. Dehydration sneaks up fast in desert heat.
🎟️ Book Fossil Creek NOW. Permits sell out weeks in advance on recreation.gov. This is the one thing that needs to be done immediately.
👟 Footwear. Grippy hiking shoes for Camelback and Devil's Bridge (* lots of rock scrambling). Water shoes or old sneakers for West Fork and The Crack.
🚗 Car rental (~$319 USD total). You'll need one — Sedona is ~2 hrs from Phoenix and most activity trailheads aren't Uber-accessible. Book early for June and make sure to pick it up at PHX right after landing.
